How to Schedule an Email
in Microsoft Outlook

Send your emails at the perfect time, every time

Know your options

2 ways to schedule emails in Outlook

Schedule Send (New Outlook and Web)

  • Click the dropdown arrow next to the Send button
  • Pick a suggested time or choose a custom date and time
  • Cloud-based -- sends even if your computer is off
  • Email stays in Drafts until it is sent

Delay Delivery (Classic Outlook)

  • Go to Options tab and click Delay Delivery
  • Check "Do not deliver before" and set a date and time
  • Outlook must stay open for the email to send
  • Email stays in Outbox until it is sent
Recommendation: If you use the New Outlook or Outlook Web, use Schedule Send -- it is cloud-based and sends reliably even if your computer is off.

New Outlook and Web

Step-by-step: Schedule Send

1

Compose your email

Write your email as usual -- add recipients, subject line, and body text.

2

Click the Send dropdown arrow

Look for the small arrow next to the Send button. Click it to open the scheduling options.

3

Select "Schedule send"

Choose one of the suggested times (like "Tomorrow morning") or click "Custom time" to pick your own date and time.

4

Click Send

The email moves to your Drafts folder and will be sent automatically at the scheduled time -- even if your computer is off.

To cancel or edit: Go to your Drafts folder, open the scheduled email, click the Edit pencil icon, then choose "Send now" or change the time.
Classic Outlook desktop

Step-by-step: Delay Delivery

1

Compose your email

Write your email as usual -- add recipients, subject line, and body text.

2

Go to Options tab

Click the "Options" tab in the ribbon at the top of your email window.

3

Click "Delay Delivery"

In the More Options group, click "Delay Delivery." A Properties dialog box will open.

4

Set the delivery time

Check "Do not deliver before" and pick the date and time you want the email sent. Click "Close."

5

Click Send

The email moves to your Outbox folder and will be sent at the scheduled time. Outlook must remain open for this to work.

Important: In Classic Outlook, your computer must stay on and Outlook must stay open for the scheduled email to send. If you close Outlook, the email will wait in the Outbox until you reopen it.
Edit or cancel

How to manage scheduled emails

New Outlook and Web

  • Go to your Drafts folder
  • Open the scheduled email (it has a clock icon)
  • Click the Edit pencil icon
  • Choose "Send now" to send immediately
  • Or change the time and click Send again
  • To cancel: click "Don't send" to move it to Deleted Items

Classic Outlook Desktop

  • Go to your Outbox folder
  • Double-click the scheduled email to open it
  • Go to Options and click Delay Delivery
  • Change the date and time, then click Close and Send
  • Or uncheck "Do not deliver before" and click Send to send now
  • To cancel: delete the email from the Outbox
Work smarter

Best practices for scheduling emails

Respect time zones

Schedule emails to arrive during the recipient's working hours, not yours. This increases the chance they read and respond quickly.

Avoid off-hours emails

Writing at 11 PM? Schedule it for 8 AM the next morning. This respects boundaries and keeps your email at the top of their inbox when they start their day.

Set a delay rule for all emails

In Classic Outlook, create a rule to delay ALL outgoing emails by 1-2 minutes. This gives you a window to catch mistakes before they send.

Double-check before scheduling

Review recipients, attachments, and content before scheduling. Once the time passes, the email is sent and cannot be recalled easily.

Fix it fast

Troubleshooting common issues

I do not see "Schedule send" next to the Send button

You may be using Classic Outlook instead of New Outlook. In Classic Outlook, use the Delay Delivery option under the Options tab instead. Also, Schedule Send is not available for POP or IMAP accounts -- it requires an Exchange or Microsoft 365 account.

My scheduled email did not send at the right time

In Classic Outlook, the app must remain open for the email to send. If you closed Outlook or shut down your computer, the email waited in the Outbox. Use New Outlook or Outlook Web for cloud-based scheduling that works even when your computer is off.

I cannot find my scheduled email

In New Outlook and Web, check your Drafts folder. In Classic Outlook, check your Outbox folder. Scheduled emails sit there until the delivery time.

The email was sent but the recipient says they got it late

Outlook schedules the send time, not the delivery time. Delays on the recipient's mail server, spam filters, or network issues can add extra time. The scheduled time is based on your time zone, not the recipient's.

I want to schedule emails to a distribution list or Microsoft 365 group

Schedule Send does not currently support sending to distribution lists or Microsoft 365 groups. Use Delay Delivery in Classic Outlook as a workaround, or send to individual recipients.
